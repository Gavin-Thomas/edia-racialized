# 01 Literature Search

> Initial landscape scoping for the EDIA Reporting in Canadian Mental Health Pharmacotherapy RCTs scoping review.

## Purpose

This folder holds the **landscape scoping** that preceded the formal database search. Its goal was to map existing literature on equity, diversity, inclusion, and accessibility (EDIA) reporting in psychiatry clinical trials, identify prior systematic/scoping reviews in adjacent areas, surface the most policy-relevant reference points, and justify the need for a Canada-specific scoping review.

## Files in this folder

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| [`landscape_report.md`](landscape_report.md) | Key prior studies (Bellato 2025 JAMA Psychiatry meta-analysis; Karran 2023 PROGRESS-Plus scoping review; Maduka 2021 JAMA Surg), preliminary search concepts, candidate databases, and controlled-vocabulary mappings (MeSH, Emtree, PsycINFO) used to seed the formal search. |

## How this stage was executed

1. **Scoping probes** of PubMed, Google Scholar, and topic-relevant journals to identify the most recent high-profile reviews on race/ethnicity reporting in psychiatry RCTs and other clinical specialties.
2. **Gap identification**: no prior review was found that (a) focused on Canadian trials, (b) applied the PROGRESS-Plus framework comprehensively, and (c) covered the post-2016 Canadian/international equity-policy period. The Bellato 2025 meta-analysis was the closest analogue but was international in scope.
3. **Concept block drafting**: three conceptual blocks (mental disorders / pharmacotherapy & RCT / Canada & EDIA) were drafted and seeded with MeSH, Emtree, and PsycINFO terms for later translation into database-specific queries.
4. **Database recommendation**: PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, PsycINFO, and CINAHL were considered. PsycINFO and CINAHL were ultimately excluded from the formal search because they lack programmatic APIs (see [`../03_inclusion_exclusion/criteria.md`](../03_inclusion_exclusion/criteria.md), "Database selection" section).

## Key decisions / rationale

- **Scoping review (not systematic review)**: the question is descriptive — characterizing reporting practices — rather than synthesizing effect estimates. A scoping review is the appropriate design.
- **Focus on racialized groups with a distinct Indigenous analysis**: motivated by Canadian equity-research norms (TCPS2 Chapter 9, CIHR SGBA+) and a gap identified in Bellato 2025, which did not disaggregate Indigenous participation.
- **2016 cut-off**: aligns with major equity-reporting policy milestones (2015 NIH inclusion policy, 2017 ClinicalTrials.gov race/ethnicity requirement, 2018 TCPS2 revision, 2019 CIHR SGBA+, 2020 anti-Black racism reckoning, 2022 CIHR EDI tightening).
