# Research Question

Among randomized controlled trials conducted in Canada evaluating pharmacological interventions to prevent, treat, or manage diagnosed mental disorders (2016-2026), what is the representation and reporting of EDIA-related metrics — with a focus on racialized groups — among participants, and how does representation vary across trials and disorders?

## Framework: PCC (Population, Concept, Context)

- **Population**: RCTs evaluating pharmacological interventions for DSM-5/ICD-10 mental disorders with at least one verified Canadian recruitment site, published 2016-2026 in English
- **Concept**: EDIA reporting practices as defined by the PROGRESS-Plus framework — specifically race/ethnicity representation, sex/gender distinction, Indigenous participation and governance, and CONSORT-Equity compliance
- **Context**: Canadian clinical trial infrastructure, benchmarked against Statistics Canada Census 2021 and compared across disorder categories, time periods (pre/post-2020), and trial characteristics (funder, multisite/single-site, sample size)

## Primary Objectives
1. Determine the proportion of trials reporting participant race/ethnicity and characterize completeness and granularity of reporting
2. Quantify representation of racialized groups among trial participants relative to Canadian population benchmarks (Statistics Canada Census 2021)
3. Identify trial-level factors associated with adequate vs. underrepresentation (funder, disorder category, trial size, publication year)

## Secondary Objectives
1. Assess how race/ethnicity variables are incorporated into trial design and analysis (descriptive only vs. covariate vs. subgroup analysis)
2. Characterize reporting of other PROGRESS-Plus variables (sex/gender distinction, SES, education, Indigenous participation, disability, SOGI)
3. Evaluate whether Indigenous peoples' participation is reported distinctly from racialized groups, and whether OCAP/community partnership principles are referenced
4. Compare EDIA reporting between pre-2020 and post-2020 publications (reflecting the increased attention to racial equity following the 2020 anti-Black racism reckoning and COVID-19 pandemic)
5. Identify structural barriers and enablers to recruitment of racialized groups as reported by trial authors

## Analytic Notes

### Temporal Comparison (Objective 4)

The pre/post-2020 comparison uses an unequal interval design: pre-2020 spans 4 years (2016–2019) and post-2020 spans 6–7 years (2020–2026). Raw counts must be reported as **proportions within each period**, not as absolute numbers.

**Sensitivity analyses for temporal cutpoints:**
The choice of 2020 as the primary cutpoint is defensible (COVID-19 pandemic; global anti-Black racism reckoning) but should be supplemented with two additional cutpoints reflecting distinct Canadian policy milestones:

| Cutpoint | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| 2020 (primary) | Anti-Black racism reckoning; COVID-19 pandemic highlighting health disparities |
| 2018 | TCPS2 revised (Chapter 9 Indigenous research strengthened; Chapter 4 equity) |
| 2022 | CIHR EDI requirements strengthened for all grant applications |

For each cutpoint, report the proportion of trials with adequate race/ethnicity reporting in the "before" vs. "after" window. If findings are consistent across all three cutpoints, the temporal effect is robust; if sensitive to cutpoint choice, report all three with appropriate caveats.

**Interrupted time series (aspirational):** With the full 10,904-record population now screened, an interrupted time series analysis (Segmented regression, Penfold & Zhang *MMWR* 2013) could be considered if sample size permits, though this would require additional included studies beyond the current 66.

## Terminology
- **Racialized groups**: People who experience racialization — the social process by which racial meaning is ascribed to groups. Race is a social construct, not a biological variable. In the Canadian context, this includes visible minorities as defined under the *Employment Equity Act* (non-Caucasian in race or non-white in colour), excluding Indigenous peoples.
- **Indigenous peoples**: First Nations, Inuit, and Metis peoples, analyzed under a distinct framework of self-determination and data sovereignty (TCPS2 Chapter 9, OCAP principles).
- This review uses "racialized groups" as the primary term (consistent with Canadian equity research norms), with "race/ethnicity" used only when describing how individual trials categorize their participants.
