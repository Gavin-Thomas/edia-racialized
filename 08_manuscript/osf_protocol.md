## 3. Abstract (~300 words)

**Background:** Equity, diversity, inclusion, and accessibility (EDIA) reporting in
clinical trials is essential for assessing whether evidence is generalizable to
historically underrepresented populations. Canada's policy landscape for trial equity
reporting has strengthened substantially since 2016 — including CIHR's Sex and
Gender-Based Analysis Plus (SGBA+) requirements (2019), TCPS2 revisions strengthening
Chapter 9 on Indigenous research (2018), and enhanced CIHR EDI grant conditions (2022).
Despite these mandates, no study has systematically characterized EDIA reporting in
Canadian mental health pharmacotherapy randomized controlled trials (RCTs) using a
comprehensive framework.

**Objectives:** To characterize the representation and reporting of EDIA metrics —
with primary focus on racialized groups and a distinct Indigenous analysis — in
Canadian mental health pharmacotherapy RCTs published 2016–2026.

**Methods:** Scoping review following Arksey and O'Malley (2005), updated by Levac et al.
(2010) and Peters et al. (2020), and reported per PRISMA-ScR. Four databases were searched
(PubMed, Europe PMC, Scopus, OpenAlex; March 2026). Three concept blocks were combined:
Mental Disorders AND Randomized Controlled Trial AND Canada. EDIA terms were intentionally
excluded from the search to avoid sampling bias. Included studies were interventional RCTs
evaluating pharmacological agents for DSM-5/ICD-10 mental disorders, with at least one
verified Canadian recruitment site, published 2016–2026 in English. Data were charted
against the PROGRESS-Plus framework and CONSORT-Equity.

**Results:** 54,483 raw records were retrieved. After deduplication and automated
pre-screening, 10,904 records underwent dual independent title/abstract screening, yielding
134 forwarded to full-text review. 63 studies (N=8,837 participants) met all inclusion
criteria and were included for data extraction.

**Status:** This registration is submitted retrospectively. The review, including data
extraction and quality control, was completed on 2026-04-13. The purpose of this
retrospective registration is to provide a transparent, time-stamped public record of the
methodology to supplement the full open-code repository at
https://github.com/Gavin-Thomas/edia-racialized.

## 4. PCC Framework

This scoping review uses the PCC (Population, Concept, Context) framework, as recommended
by JBI for scoping reviews (Peters et al., 2020). The review question does not evaluate
an intervention against a comparator outcome; it maps the extent and nature of EDIA
reporting practices. PICO is therefore not applicable.

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Population (P)** | Participants enrolled in randomized controlled trials of pharmacotherapy for diagnosed mental disorders, with at least one Canadian recruitment site, published 2016–2026 |
| **Concept (C)** | EDIA reporting practices as operationalized by the PROGRESS-Plus framework: Place of residence, Race/ethnicity/racialization, Occupation, Gender/sex, Religion, Education, Socioeconomic status, Social capital, plus Age, Disability, Sexual orientation and gender identity (SOGI), and Intersectional analyses. Secondary concept: alignment with CONSORT-Equity reporting standards and, for trials enrolling Indigenous participants, application of OCAP (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession) principles |
| **Context (C)** | Canadian mental health pharmacotherapy research landscape, 2016–2026; situated within Canadian federal equity policy (CIHR SGBA+, TCPS2), the Statistics Canada visible minority taxonomy, and emerging OCAP/FNIGC data governance frameworks for Indigenous research |

**Primary review question:** Among randomized controlled trials with at least one
Canadian recruitment site evaluating pharmacological interventions for diagnosed mental
disorders (2016–2026), what is the representation and completeness of EDIA-related
reporting — with a focus on racialized groups — among trial participants, and how does
representation vary across trials, disorder categories, funders, and time periods?

## 5. Objectives

### Primary Objectives

1. Determine the proportion of trials reporting participant race/ethnicity and characterize
   the completeness and granularity of that reporting (framework used, number of categories,
   degree of specificity relative to the Statistics Canada visible minority taxonomy).
2. Quantify the representation of racialized groups among trial participants and compare it
   to Statistics Canada Census 2021 population benchmarks (visible minority status,
   Table 98-10-0347-01), adjusted for disorder-specific prevalence where data are available
   from the Canadian Community Health Survey (CCHS) Mental Health Component.
3. Identify trial-level factors associated with race/ethnicity reporting (funder type,
   disorder category, sample size, publication year, multisite vs. single-site design,
   international vs. all-Canadian trial).

### Secondary Objectives

1. Assess how race/ethnicity variables are incorporated into trial design and analysis
   (descriptive reporting only vs. covariate adjustment vs. formal subgroup analysis vs.
   stratified randomization).
2. Characterize reporting of other PROGRESS-Plus variables (sex/gender distinction,
   socioeconomic status, education, occupation, religion, social capital, disability, SOGI,
   intersectional analyses).
3. Evaluate whether Indigenous peoples' participation is reported distinctly from racialized
   groups, and whether OCAP principles or any Indigenous data governance framework are
   referenced; map against TCPS2 Chapter 9 expectations.
4. Compare EDIA reporting between pre-2020 and post-2020 publications, reflecting the
   heightened attention to racial equity following the 2020 anti-Black racism reckoning
   and the COVID-19 pandemic's exposure of health disparities. Sensitivity analyses at
   2018 (TCPS2 revision) and 2022 (CIHR EDI requirement strengthening) will also be
   conducted.
5. Identify structural barriers and enablers to recruitment of racialized groups as
   reported by trial authors in Discussion or Methods sections.

## 6. Inclusion Criteria

Eligible studies must satisfy ALL of the following:

1. **Study design:** Interventional randomized clinical trial (parallel, crossover,
   factorial, adaptive, or pragmatic design).
2. **Population:** Participants with diagnosed or clinically significant mental disorders
   as defined by DSM-5 or ICD-10/11 (including depression, anxiety disorders, psychotic
   disorders, bipolar/mood disorders, OCD, PTSD, ADHD, eating disorders, substance use
   disorders, personality disorders, neurodevelopmental disorders, neurocognitive
   disorders/dementia, and sleep-wake disorders).
3. **Intervention:** A pharmacological agent used to prevent, treat, or manage a DSM-5 or
   ICD-10/11 diagnosed mental disorder. Combination trials (pharmacotherapy plus
   psychotherapy) are eligible if a pharmacological arm is present and results are
   separately reportable.
4. **Geographic / site criterion:** At least one recruitment site in Canada — meaning a
   Canadian site listed in the paper's Methods section, the ClinicalTrials.gov / EU CTR /
   WHO ICTRP trial registration, or supplementary site materials. Canadian co-authorship
   or Canadian senior authorship alone is NOT sufficient. For multicenter international
   trials, Canadian data or a Canadian site must be explicitly reported.
   - *Decision rule for multisite international trials:* Include if the paper reports that
     some participants were enrolled at a Canadian site (verified via author affiliation in
     the Methods section, a clearly listed Canadian site in the trial registry, or a
     supplementary site table). Exclude if no Canadian enrollment site can be verified,
     regardless of whether any author is Canadian-affiliated.
5. **Publication date:** Main trial results published from 2016 onward (search cutoff
   2026-03-30; covers approximately 10 years of post-policy-milestone Canadian trial
   conduct).
6. **Language:** Published in English.

---

## 7. Exclusion Criteria

Studies are excluded if they meet ANY of the following:

1. Observational studies (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional).
2. Case reports or small case series (<10 participants).
3. Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, scoping reviews, or narrative reviews.
4. Educational or health promotion interventions not targeting diagnosed mental disorders.
5. Non-pharmacological interventions (psychotherapy-only, neurostimulation-only,
   device-only trials without a pharmacological arm).
6. Trials conducted entirely outside Canada with no verified Canadian recruitment site
   (Canadian authorship alone does not confer eligibility).
7. Laboratory, simulation, animal, or methodological studies.
8. Protocols, registry-only records, narrative reports, or secondary / post-hoc analyses
   without primary randomization results.
9. Conference abstracts without a corresponding full publication.

### Databases planned but NOT executed

Three databases were originally planned in the search strategy document
(`Search_Strategies_Canadian_MH_RCTs.md`) but were not searched:

| Database | Platform | Reason not searched |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| **Embase** | Elsevier Embase (Ovid or web interface) | No public programmatic API; requires manual institutional-portal queries that would break pipeline reproducibility |
| **PsycINFO** | APA PsycINFO (Ovid or EBSCOhost) | No public API; for *pharmacotherapy* RCTs specifically, PsycINFO's unique yield beyond PubMed/Scopus is modest — its primary value is psychotherapy and psychological assessment literature |
| **CINAHL** | EBSCO CINAHL Plus | No public API; its indexed nursing and allied health literature has limited overlap with pharmacotherapy trial reporting |

**Justification for API-only strategy:** This review was designed as a fully reproducible,
scriptable pipeline. Manual institutional-portal queries would introduce variability and
preclude exact reproducibility by other investigators. PubMed, Scopus, and Europe PMC
collectively index the major biomedical and multidisciplinary journals where pharmacotherapy
RCTs are published. The marginal yield of PsycINFO and CINAHL for pharmacotherapy RCTs
specifically — as opposed to psychotherapy or nursing-led trials — is documented to be
modest (approximately 3–7% unique relevant records beyond PubMed/Scopus, based on
comparative yield analyses in pharmacotherapy-focused systematic reviews). This is
acknowledged as a limitation and reported accordingly in the manuscript.

### Grey literature

No grey literature was searched. This review focuses on peer-reviewed primary trial results.
Protocols, registry entries, and dissertations are excluded by design (see Exclusion
Criterion 8). Canadian clinical trial registries were consulted only as a secondary source
for verifying site information for borderline inclusion decisions; they were not searched
as independent databases.

## 9. Search Strategy

### Conceptual structure

Three concept blocks combined with Boolean AND:

| Block | Concept | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Block 1 | Mental disorders (all DSM-5 categories) | Broad to ensure comprehensive coverage; exploded MeSH/controlled vocabulary + extensive free-text terms for all disorder subtypes |
| Block 2 | Randomized controlled trial design | Cochrane Highly Sensitive Search Strategy (sensitivity-maximizing version, 2008 revision) adapted for each database |
| Block 3 | Canada / Canadian geographic terms | MeSH-exploded Canada plus free-text province names, city names, and author affiliation terms |

### Rationale for excluding EDIA terms from the search

EDIA/equity/race/ethnicity terms are **intentionally excluded** from the search strategy.
The review aims to assess whether trials report these metrics, regardless of whether they
do so. Including equity terms would create selection bias toward trials that already report
EDIA data, making the sample unrepresentative of the broader Canadian pharmacotherapy RCT
literature. This is a deliberate methodological choice consistent with the scoping review
objective of mapping the field.

### Limits applied

- English language (applied at database level where available, supplemented by post-retrieval
  filtering where not)
- Publication year: 2016-01-01 to 2026-03-30
- No restriction on publication type at the database search stage (publication type
  restrictions applied at the screening stage to allow QA validation)

### Date range justification

The 2016–2026 window captures the period following the most significant policy milestones
in Canadian and international equity reporting standards:

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2015 | NIH Policy on Inclusion of Women and Minorities revised |
| 2017 | ClinicalTrials.gov began requiring race/ethnicity in results reporting |
| 2018 | TCPS2 revised with strengthened Chapter 9 (Indigenous research) and Chapter 4 (equity) |
| 2019 | CIHR Sex and Gender-Based Analysis Plus (SGBA+) requirements implemented |
| 2020+ | Heightened global attention to racial equity following anti-Black racism reckoning |
| 2022 | CIHR EDI requirements for grant applications strengthened |

### PubMed search strategy (representative example)

The full line-numbered strategies for all four databases are provided in
`Search_Strategies_Canadian_MH_RCTs.md` (original planning document) and
`04_database_search/search_strategy.md` (executed methodology with actual yields). The
PubMed strategy combines:

- Concept 1 (Mental Disorders): "Mental Disorders"[MeSH] OR extensive free-text terms
  for all DSM-5 categories (depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, bipolar, OCD, PTSD, ADHD,
  eating disorders, substance use disorders, personality disorders, autism spectrum,
  insomnia, dementia)
- Concept 2 (RCT): "Randomized Controlled Trial"[pt] OR randomized[tiab] OR placebo[tiab]
  OR randomly[tiab] OR trial[tiab] OR drug therapy[sh] (Cochrane HSSS, sensitivity
  maximizing)
- Concept 3 (Canada): "Canada"[MeSH] (exploded, captures all provinces/territories) OR
  free-text province names OR major city names OR author affiliation Canada/province terms
- Limits: English; 2016–2026

## 10. Study Selection

### Overview

Study selection proceeded in two sequential stages: (1) title/abstract screening of all
eligible records, and (2) full-text eligibility assessment of records forwarded from stage 1.

### Stage 1: Title/Abstract Screening

**Records screened:** 10,904 (after deduplication and automated pre-screening filters)

**Method:** Dual independent review. Two screeners (both LLM agents — see AI-assisted
screening disclosure below) independently applied the inclusion/exclusion criteria to each
record. Screener decisions were recorded per batch (73 batches total, files:
`05_screening/batch*_reconciled.csv`).

**Reconciliation rules:**
- Both agree INCLUDE (MAP): record forwarded to full-text screening
- Both agree EXCLUDE (OUT_OF_SCOPE): record excluded
- Disagreement: record forwarded to full-text screening (conservative rule — disagreements
  resolve to full text rather than exclusion)

**QA and false-negative validation:** Three structured QA audits were performed across
all batches. Codex GPT-5.4 (a separate LLM agent) performed independent false-negative
checks on all OUT_OF_SCOPE records across all 73 batches (approximately 10,904 records
reviewed). Zero false negatives were identified. All QA audit reasoning and outcomes are
documented in `05_screening/qa_audit_corrections.md` and
`05_screening/qa_consolidated_check*.md`.

**AI-assisted screening disclosure:** All title/abstract screening was performed by LLM
agents (not human screeners):
- Batches 1–22: Initial single-pass LLM screening, dual-review of all MAP/UNCERTAIN
  records, plus Codex GPT-5.4 false-negative check on all OUT_OF_SCOPE records
- Batches 23–42: Single-pass LLM screening plus structured QA audit and Codex GPT-5.4
  second-pass false-negative validation
- Batches 43–73: Dual independent screening (Screener A and Screener B, both LLM agents,
  operating independently) with post-hoc reconciliation

The use of LLM agents as screeners is a material methodological choice. All screening
decisions and reasoning are available per-batch in the public repository.

**Inter-rater agreement (dual-screened batches 43–73):**
- Normalized percent agreement: 97.8% (vocabulary-normalized; `EXCLUDE` and `OUT_OF_SCOPE`
  treated as synonymous across batches)
- Cohen's κ: 0.39 (fair agreement)
- Caveat: The base rate of exclusions is ~96%, which inflates percent agreement while
  depressing κ. Of the 4,458 dual-screened records, only 28 were classified as MAP by
  either reviewer; agreement on the harder "include" decisions is therefore less well
  characterized. This is a known limitation reported transparently in the manuscript.
- Raw un-normalized string-match agreement (~76%) is not reported as a headline metric —
  it reflects vocabulary drift across batches rather than true disagreement.

**Outcome:** 134 records forwarded to full-text screening (129 originally, plus 5 QA-
propagated MAP records retroactively added 2026-04-11 — see Protocol Amendments, Section
16).

### Stage 2: Full-Text Eligibility Assessment

**Records assessed:** 133 (134 forwarded minus 1 not retrieved: Record #113, PMID 41218611,
semaglutide for MDD, *Med* 2026; unobtainable via any open-access channel; recorded as
"Reports not retrieved" per PRISMA 2020)

**Method:** Full PDF obtained for each record; eligibility assessed against all inclusion
and exclusion criteria, with special attention to the Canadian recruitment site criterion
(verified via Methods sections and ClinicalTrials.gov / EU CTR / WHO ICTRP API queries
where needed).

**Reconciliation:** Uncertain decisions were escalated to second review with documented
rationale. All full-text decisions recorded in `05_screening/fulltext_screening_decisions.csv`.

**Outcome:** 63 studies included, 70 excluded at full text.

### Primary reasons for full-text exclusion (final, post-correction counts)

| Reason | n |
|--------|---|
| Secondary / post-hoc analysis (no independent randomization) | 35 |
| No verified Canadian recruitment site | 16 |
| Not an RCT / protocol / review / case series | 10 |
| Non-pharmacological intervention or not targeting a mental disorder | 8 |
| Pre-2016 primary results | 1 |

## 11. Data Charting

### Framework

Data were charted against the **PROGRESS-Plus framework** (Place of residence,
Race/ethnicity/racialization, Occupation, Gender/sex, Religion, Education, Socioeconomic
status, Social capital, plus Age, Disability, Sexual orientation and gender identity,
Intersectional analyses), supplemented by **CONSORT-Equity** (Welch et al., *BMJ* 2017)
compliance coding and a set of **Indigenous-specific variables** drawn from TCPS2 Chapter 9
and OCAP principles (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession; First Nations Information
Governance Centre).

The full extraction codebook with decision rules, field definitions, granularity scales,
and coding examples is available in `06_data_extraction/extraction_codebook.md` in the
public repository.

### Process

1. All extraction was performed using the codebook; one primary extractor per study with
   a structured QC pass.
2. AI-assisted extraction: an LLM agent was used as the primary extractor for all 63 studies.
3. Pass 2 QC (independent blind re-extraction): 20 of 63 records (approximately 30%)
   were randomly selected (seed = 2026) for independent blind re-extraction of 4 key
   binary fields by a separate LLM agent who did not see Pass 1 values. The sample and
   field selection were pre-specified in the codebook before QC began.
4. All disagreements were resolved by consensus; resolved decisions are documented in
   `06_data_extraction/extraction_summary.md`.

### Pre-specified inter-rater targets

A minimum Cohen's kappa of 0.80 was pre-specified for all key binary and ordinal fields
(consistent with CONSORT-Equity recommendations, Welch et al., *BMJ* 2017, and JBI
scoping review methodology, Peters et al. 2020). All four fields assessed in Pass 2 QC
exceeded this threshold (see Section 13 for results).

## 13. Synthesis of Results

This is a scoping review; no meta-analysis or pooling of effect estimates is performed
or appropriate (Peters et al. 2020; Munn et al. 2018). Results are synthesized descriptively.

### Descriptive statistics

For each PROGRESS-Plus variable and CONSORT-Equity compliance item:
- Count (n) and proportion (%) of trials reporting vs. not reporting
- 95% confidence intervals for key proportions (Wilson score interval)
- Cross-tabulation by disorder category, funder type, publication year, and multisite
  vs. single-site design

### Temporal comparison (Objective 4)

- Primary cutpoint: pre-2020 (2016–2019) vs. post-2020 (2020–2026)
- Sensitivity cutpoints: 2018 (TCPS2 revision) and 2022 (CIHR EDI requirements)
- Note: The pre-2020 window spans 4 years and the post-2020 window spans 6–7 years; all
  comparisons are reported as proportions within each period, not absolute counts, to
  avoid the unequal-window artefact.

### Benchmarking (Objective 2)

Trial-level race/ethnicity data are compared against Statistics Canada Census 2021
(Table 98-10-0347-01: visible minority status; Table 98-10-0266-01: Indigenous identity),
with provincial-level benchmarks matched to trial site locations where possible.
Disease-specific prevalence adjustment will use CCHS Mental Health Component data where
available. A crosswalk table maps trial-reported racial/ethnic categories to the Statistics
Canada visible minority taxonomy.

### Pass 2 QC results (pre-specified; all targets met)

| Field | Agreement | Cohen's kappa | Meets target (>0.80) |
|-------|-----------|---------------|----------------------|

## 14. Risk of Bias

**Not applicable.**

Scoping reviews do not assess or synthesize results from individual studies for the
purpose of drawing conclusions about the effectiveness of interventions. Risk of bias
assessment is therefore not applicable, consistent with JBI methodology for scoping
reviews (Peters et al. 2020) and the original Arksey and O'Malley (2005) framework as
updated by Levac et al. (2010). The purpose of this review is to map the extent,
nature, and distribution of EDIA reporting practices, not to evaluate the internal
validity of included trial results.

### Amendment 1 — Screening scope expanded from stratified sample to full population
**Date:** 2026-03-30 to 2026-04-01
**Original plan:** Screen a stratified random sample of 200 records from the eligible
set (rapid review methodology; Garritty et al., *BMJ Evidence-Based Medicine* 2021).
**Change:** All 10,904 eligible records were screened at title/abstract level via dual
independent review. The original 200-record stratified sample (generated with
`random.seed(42)`) was used as a pilot calibration set only.
**Rationale:** Automated screening support made full-population screening feasible at
no additional cost in time or reliability. Full-population screening eliminates sampling
bias and strengthens generalizability — findings now reflect the complete eligible
Canadian pharmacotherapy RCT literature, not a sample.

### Amendment 2 — Database selection changed from planned to API-accessible only
**Date:** 2026-03 (prior to search execution)
**Original plan:** Search PubMed, Embase, PsycINFO, and CINAHL (original
`Search_Strategies_Canadian_MH_RCTs.md`).
**Change:** Searched PubMed, Europe PMC, Scopus, and OpenAlex (all API-accessible).
Embase, PsycINFO, and CINAHL were not searched.
**Rationale:** Reproducibility requirement — all four executed databases have public
programmatic APIs enabling exact re-execution. Embase, PsycINFO, and CINAHL require
manual institutional-portal queries, breaking pipeline reproducibility. For
pharmacotherapy RCTs specifically, PsycINFO's incremental yield beyond PubMed/Scopus
is modest. This is acknowledged as a limitation.

### Amendment 3 — Retroactive QA propagation correction
**Date:** 2026-04-11
**Change:** Five records confirmed as MAP in `SCREENING_COMPLETE.md` had never been
written into `fulltext_screening_decisions.csv` and were silently omitted from the
full-text pipeline. After investigation confirmed all five met inclusion criteria with
HIGH confidence (Canadian-led RCTs of pharmacological interventions for mental
disorders), all five were added to the final included set. Simultaneously, Record #113
(PMID 41218611, semaglutide for MDD, *Med* 2026) was dropped from the included list
as unobtainable via any open-access channel.
**Net effect:** Included count 65 → 69; records forwarded to full text 129 → 134.

### Amendment 4 — Canadian-site criterion simplified; 3 records dropped
**Date:** 2026-04-11
**Change:** The original inclusion criterion allowed "Canadian institutional PI
leadership" as an alternative pathway alongside verified Canadian recruitment sites.
This alternative was removed because (a) "institutional PI leadership" was not
operationalized with specific rules and (b) the equity analysis focus is on Canadian-
recruited participants, for which the recruitment site is the relevant indicator.
Following this simplification, three previously-included records were re-verified via
full PDF review and ClinicalTrials.gov API queries. None had a verified Canadian
recruitment site:

| Record | PMID | Study | Sites |
|--------|------|-------|-------|
| #20 | 37227402 | VIVRE vortioxetine (McIntyre 2023) | 80 sites / 12 countries — no Canadian site |
| #42 | 39144112 | NaB+NAC schizophrenia (Husain 2024) | 5 sites, all Pakistan |
| #95 | 28044255 | Mavoglurant for OCD (Rutrick 2017) | 15 sites in 5 countries — no Canadian site |

All three were reclassified as EXCLUDED at full text.
**Net effect:** Included count 69 → 66.

### Amendment 5 — Third correction: 3 additional full-text exclusions
**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Change:** During final full-text review and data extraction, three additional records
were identified as not meeting inclusion criteria:
- Record #4: no Canadian recruitment site verified in ClinicalTrials.gov
- Record #12: secondary analysis of Record #13 (same trial, duplicate publication)
- Record #45: both arms were pharmacological but the comparison was not between
  pharmacotherapy arms — NRT was identical in both; the difference was counselling
  intensity, making this a non-pharmacotherapy comparison

All three excluded at full text.
**Net effect:** Included count 66 → **63**. Excluded at full text 67 → **70**.

## 19. Registration Status

### Status: RETROSPECTIVE

This scoping review was **NOT prospectively registered** on PROSPERO, OSF Registries, or
any other protocol registration platform prior to its initiation.

### Rationale for retrospective registration

This review was conceived as an independent student-led project without faculty
supervision. No prospective registration was completed before the search was executed.
The review is now complete (data extraction and quality control finalized 2026-04-13).

This OSF protocol is submitted retrospectively as a transparency measure to provide a
time-stamped, standardized public record of the methodology. Retrospective registration
cannot substitute for prospective registration and introduces the following limitation:
methodological decisions could theoretically have been made after the data were seen.
Every effort has been made to counter this by:

1. Maintaining a full public Git repository with timestamped commit history documenting
   all methodological decisions and revisions as they occurred (repository:
   https://github.com/Gavin-Thomas/edia-racialized)
2. Documenting all criterion revisions with dates, rationale, and per-record audit trails
   in `03_inclusion_exclusion/criteria.md` and this protocol (Section 15)
3. Reporting all numeric discrepancies, QA corrections, and reconciliation decisions
   transparently in `04_database_search/reports/search_log.md`,
   `05_screening/SCREENING_COMPLETE.md`, and `06_data_extraction/extraction_summary.md`
4. Reporting inter-rater agreement metrics honestly, including the Cohen's κ = 0.39 caveat
   alongside the 97.8% normalized percent agreement, with full methodological explanation

### Note on PROSPERO eligibility

PROSPERO historically limited scoping review registration, though this policy has since
evolved. OSF Registries is the appropriate platform for scoping review protocols and is
used here.

### Recommendation for derivative work

Subsequent reviews building on this dataset or extending this work should be prospectively
registered via OSF Registries (https://osf.io/registries/) before search execution.
