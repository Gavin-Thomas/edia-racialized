TITLE: Escitalopram for agitation in Alzheimer's dementia: a randomized controlled phase 3 trial (S-CitAD)

METHODS:
A double-masked, multicenter randomized controlled trial (S-CitAD). Participants received 3 weeks of psychosocial intervention (PSI) alone, then non-responders were randomized 1:1 to escitalopram (up to 15 mg daily) or placebo for 12 weeks while continuing PSI. Study included 27 community-based centers across North America with assessments at enrollment, post-PSI baseline, and weeks 3, 6, 9, and 12.

RESULTS:
Of 243 consented participants, 205 were enrolled and 173 randomized (84 escitalopram, 89 placebo). The primary outcome showed no significant difference: the risk difference (RD) at 12 weeks was not statistically significant (unadjusted RD = -0.08; 95% confidence interval (CI): -0.21, 0.06). Secondary outcomes showed no significant differences except NPI-C agitation/aggression at 3 weeks favoring escitalopram. Safety concerns included increased QT prolongation and falls in the escitalopram group.
