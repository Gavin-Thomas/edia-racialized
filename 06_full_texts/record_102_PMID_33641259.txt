TITLE: Pharmacogenomics guided versus standard antidepressant treatment in a community pharmacy setting: A randomized controlled trial

FUNDING: Green Shield Canada, a nonprofit organization, provided operating funds toward study execution.

METHODS:
A prospective, single-blind randomized controlled design with 213 outpatients diagnosed with major depressive disorder and/or generalized anxiety disorder across three community pharmacies in Ontario. Participants were randomized to pharmacogenomics-guided treatment or standard treatment. Clinical outcomes measured included PHQ-9 (depression), GAD-7 (anxiety), and SDS (disability). Follow-up over 6 months.

RESULTS:
All clinical outcomes demonstrated significant improvements over the 6-month period. The pharmacogenomics-guided group showed greater improvements in depression (PHQ-9), anxiety (GAD-7), and disability (SDS) compared to standard treatment. Treatment satisfaction improved similarly in both groups.
