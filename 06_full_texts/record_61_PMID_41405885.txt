TITLE: Comparison of Extended-Release Buprenorphine Doses for Treating High-Risk Opioid Use: A Randomized Clinical Trial

NOTE: This article reports the MAINTENANCE PHASE results from the same trial as Record #110 (which reports the INDUCTION PHASE). Both share trial registration NCT04995029.

METHODS:
Multicenter, randomized, double-blind clinical trial conducted at 28 treatment centers in the US and Canada from October 26, 2021, to June 26, 2024. Eligible participants were aged 18+ with moderate or severe opioid use disorder for >=90 days and high-risk opioid use (injecting, high-dose, or fentanyl use) >=5 days/week for 4 weeks prior. Following an open-label induction substudy with rapid or standard buprenorphine induction and two 300-mg extended-release buprenorphine (BUP-XR) injections, participants were randomized 1:1 to receive 8 additional maintenance doses of either 100 mg or 300 mg every 4 weeks. Primary endpoint: proportion of responders for weekly opioid use, defined as participants whose percentage of visits with opioid abstinence was 80% or more over weeks 20 to 38. Assessments included urine drug screenings (UDS), self-reported drug use, and safety evaluations. Analysis followed intent-to-treat methodology with stratified randomization by injection route at screening and week 6 UDS results.

RESULTS:
Of 436 randomized participants, 435 received injections (218 in 100-mg arm, 217 in 300-mg arm; mean age 41.6 years, 57% male). Both dosing regimens improved opioid abstinence: 44 of 218 responders (20.2%) in the 100-mg arm and 50 of 216 responders (23.2%) in the 300-mg arm (Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel difference, 2.6%; 95% CI, -4.7% to 9.9%). Opioid frequency decreased from more than 43 instances at screening to fewer than 3 instances by week 3 (through week 38). Post hoc analyses revealed the 300-mg dose performed significantly better for participants with: daily fentanyl use (11.1% difference), fentanyl use >=14 times weekly (12.2% difference), or both (15.4% difference). Both doses were well tolerated without any new safety signals. Injection-site reactions were higher in the 300-mg arm (difference, 9.2%; 95% CI, 3.7%-15.2%).
