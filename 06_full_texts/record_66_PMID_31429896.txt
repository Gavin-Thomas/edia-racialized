TITLE: Effect of Continuing Olanzapine vs Placebo on Relapse Among Patients With Psychotic Depression in Remission: The STOP-PD II Randomized Clinical Trial

METHODS:
A 36-week randomized clinical trial conducted at 4 academic medical centers from November 2011 to June 2017. The study had three phases: acute, stabilization, and randomization. Participants aged 18-85 with psychotic depression received open-label sertraline (150-200 mg/d) plus olanzapine (15-20 mg/d) initially. After meeting remission criteria, they entered an 8-week stabilization phase, then randomization to either continue olanzapine or switch to placebo (4-week taper) while maintaining sertraline. Primary outcome was relapse risk; secondary outcomes included anthropometric and metabolic measures. Statistical analyses used Cox proportional hazards models for relapse and linear mixed models for metabolic measures.

RESULTS:
Among 126 randomized participants (64 olanzapine, 62 placebo), 13 (20.3%) in the olanzapine group and 34 (54.8%) in the placebo group experienced relapse (hazard ratio 0.25, 95% CI 0.13-0.48, P<.001). Number needed to treat was 2.8. Olanzapine demonstrated significantly greater daily rate increases for weight (0.13 lb, p<.001), waist circumference (0.009 inches, p=.002), and total cholesterol (0.29 mg/dL, p=.003), but no significant differences in glucose, HbA1c, or triglycerides between groups.
