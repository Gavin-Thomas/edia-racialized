TITLE: Cognitive Behavioral Insomnia Therapy for Those With Insomnia and Depression: A Randomized Controlled Clinical Trial

METHODS:
Randomized split-plot experimental design with three between-group cells comparing CBT-I + antidepressant (escitalopram 10mg), CBT-I + placebo, and antidepressant + sleep hygiene control. Study sites were Duke University (2008-2009) and Ryerson University in Toronto, Canada (2009-2014). Participants (107 total, ages 18-64) met criteria for major depressive disorder and insomnia disorder. The treatment involved four biweekly 1-hour sessions over 8 weeks. Primary outcomes included sleep diary measures (total wake time and sleep efficiency) and polysomnographic monitoring at baseline and post-treatment.

RESULTS:
All groups improved significantly on subjective sleep measures from baseline to post-treatment with large effect sizes. For objective sleep (PSG), both CBT groups improved on TWT (p = .03), but the AD + SH group worsened. No statistically significant group differences emerged for PSG sleep efficiency (p = .07). All groups showed significant depression improvement on the Hamilton Rating Scale for Depression with no between-group differences. Post-treatment insomnia severity predicted depression outcomes.
