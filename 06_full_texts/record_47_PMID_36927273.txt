TITLE: An alpha7 nAChR approach for the baseline-dependent modulation of deviance detection in schizophrenia: A pilot study assessing the combined effect of CDP-choline and galantamine

METHODS:
Study participants: Twenty-six chronic schizophrenia patients were recruited from the Schizophrenia Outpatient Program at The Royal Ottawa Mental Health Centre. Eligible participants had a schizophrenia diagnosis based on DSM-IV with clinically stable status for minimum 90 days prior to recruitment, assessed with the positive and negative symptom scale (PANSS). Recruited patients were on stable neuroleptic regimen for at least 4 weeks consisting of one or combination of treatments: aripiprazole, risperidone, flupentixol, haloperidol, paliperidone, levomepromazine, olanzapine, perphenazine, quetiapine, trifluoperazine, ziprasidone. Individuals taking clozapine were excluded. Study exclusion factors included other DSM-IV disorders, medical illness, recent (<6 months) head injury with loss of consciousness, and hearing impairment (measured audiometrically). All study procedures complied with Research Ethics Boards of The Royal Ottawa Mental Health Care Group and University of Ottawa. Participants signed informed consent and received $100 upon completion. Final study sample consisted of 24 participants (7 females, 17 males; 8 non-smokers, 16 smokers; mean age 44.5 years, SE +/-2.06). One participant dropped out before completion; another's data discarded due to significant EEG artifacts. Non-smoking status relied on participant attestation of fewer than 100 lifetime cigarettes and no tobacco use within past year. Smokers averaged 24.3 years (SE +/-2.0) of smoking, consuming approximately 16.6 cigarettes daily (SE +/-1.9).

Experimental design: A randomized, double-blinded, counterbalanced design assessed volunteers during placebo and active treatment (CDP-choline + galantamine) sessions. Equal numbers started with placebo versus treatment. MMN amplitude measures from baseline placebo condition stratified total sample into low (LG), medium (MG), and high (HG) amplitude individuals using frontal (Fz) electrode measurements.

Treatment administration: Non-active placebo capsules (250 mg cellulose) matched active treatment capsules (2 x 250 mg CDP-choline capsules plus 1 x 250 mg capsule containing 16 mg galantamine + 234 mg cellulose) in shape, size, and colour. Participants ingested two capsules at time 0 minutes and single capsule 60 minutes later.

Experimental procedure: Two testing sessions started between 8:00 am and 11:30 am. Participants verbally confirmed abstinence from food, drugs (neuroleptics taken as usual), alcohol, caffeine, and tobacco. Smoking abstinence verified by exhaled carbon monoxide measurement. Participants orally consumed capsules (placebo or CDP-choline) at time 0. They remained seated in sound-attenuated chamber watching emotionally neutral movie during treatment absorption. After 60 minutes, second treatment administered; absorption continued additional 60 minutes. MMN paradigm presentation began at peak CDP-choline/galantamine absorption (120 minutes).

MMN paradigm: Finnish-language consonant-vowel stimuli (/te:/ and /pi:/) projected binaurally through noise-canceling headphones. Deviant stimuli differed from standard in syllable frequency (FRE), intensity (INT), vowel duration (DUR), consonant change (CON), or vowel change (VOW). Paradigm presented in four blocks. Following optimum-1 MMN paradigm recommendations, each 5-minute block included 465 syllables (1860 total stimuli) where every other syllable was standard (p = 0.5) with one of five deviant syllables (p = 0.1 each).

ERP recording and processing: Brain Vision Quickamp amplifier with Brain Vision Recorder software recorded EEG signals. Bandpass filter set 0.1-70 Hz with 500 Hz continuous digitization. 30 Ag+/AgCl electrodes positioned at scalp sites based on 10/10 international system. Greatest negative peak occurring 100-250 ms post-stimulus quantified MMN peak amplitude and latency at frontal sites (Fz, F3, F4).

Clinical measures: PANSS positive symptoms (score range 7-49), negative symptoms (range 7-49), general psychopathology (range 16-112). Sum of three subscales composed PANSS total score (M = 62.25, SE +/-2.8).

Statistical analysis: Frontal (Fz) MMN amplitude measures analyzed using SPSS. MMN amplitudes to each deviant type analyzed separately. Overall active treatment effect analyzed using repeated measures ANOVA. Group stratification served as between-subjects factor in repeated measures ANOVAs. Chlorpromazine equivalent doses assessed for group differences. Between-group differences assessed using Fisher's exact test and one-way ANOVAs. Spearman's correlations examined treatment-induced MMN change relationships with PANSS scores.

RESULTS:
Demographic and clinical measures: Twenty-four schizophrenia participants (M age = 44.5 years, SE +/-2.06; 7 females, 17 males; 8 non-smokers, 16 smokers) included in final sample. LG, MG, HG subgroups not statistically distinguished by age, gender, or smoking status. Subgroups scored similarly on PANSS.

MMN amplitude measures - Total group: No main drug effect observed in total group for any MMN deviants.

Group comparisons: No main drug effect for any deviants. As expected, main group effects observed for all deviant types showing significant amplitude differences among LG, MG, HG. Significant treatment x group interactions observed for frequency (F(2,21) = 9.72, p = 0.001), duration (F(2,21) = 11.19, p < 0.001), consonant (F(2,21) = 6.30, p = 0.07), and vowel (F(2,21) = 10.24, p = 0.001) deviants. CDP-choline/galantamine significantly increased MMN amplitudes in LG group for frequency, duration, and vowel deviants. Opposite effect observed for HG group with CDP-choline/galantamine showing smaller amplitudes compared to placebo. No treatment effect for medium group.

CDP-choline/galantamine-based subgrouping: No significant findings when subgrouping based on active treatment session scores.

Correlations: Treatment-induced amplitude changes negatively correlated with placebo amplitudes for all MMN deviants. Consonant amplitude treatment change scores correlated with PANSS total score (rho = -0.447, p = 0.028) and PANSS negative subscale (rho = -0.414, p = 0.044). Vowel amplitude change correlated with PANSS total (rho = -0.522, p = 0.009) and PANSS general subscale (rho = -0.475, p = 0.019).

Adverse events: No significant differences during CDP-choline/galantamine treatment compared to placebo. No severe symptom reports.

Vital signs: Blood pressure and heart rate not significantly different between conditions.
