TITLE: Comparative effectiveness research trial for antidepressant incomplete and non-responders with treatment resistant depression (ASCERTAIN-TRD) a randomized clinical trial

METHODS:
This was a multi-site, eight-week, randomized rater-blinded trial comparing three treatment arms for MDD patients with TRD who are currently on ongoing, stable and adequate antidepressant therapy (ADT): a) aripiprazole augmentation, b) rTMS augmentation, and c) switching to venlafaxine XR (or duloxetine for patients who had received venlafaxine during their current major depressive episode). Subjects aged 18-80 with MDD lasting >=12 weeks and MADRS scores >=20 were included if they met TRD criteria (nonresponse to >=2 adequate antidepressant trials). Randomization occurred 1:1:1 across 17 U.S. and Canadian sites. Post-baseline visits occurred at weeks 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 8. Montgomery-Asberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS) assessments were performed by blinded raters, with self-rated symptoms of depression questionnaire (SDQ) administered at each visit. Primary outcome was MADRS change. Mixed-effects models with repeated measures (MMRM) analyzed efficacy using modified intent-to-treat dataset with Bonferroni-corrected alpha of 0.025.

RESULTS:
278 participants were randomized; 260 had >=1 post-baseline MADRS assessment. Repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation (score change (standard error (se)) = -17.39 (1.3) (p = 0.015) but not aripiprazole augmentation (score change (se) = -14.9 (1.1) (p = 0.069) was superior to switch. For SDQ, aripiprazole (mean change (se) = -37.79 (2.9) (p = 0.003) but not repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation augmentation (mean change (se) = -42.96 (3.6) (p = 0.031) was superior to switch. MADRS response rates: rTMS 52.2% vs. switch 35.8% (NNT=7, p=0.038); aripiprazole 38.1% vs. switch 35.8% (p=0.743). No serious drug or rTMS-related adverse events occurred.

EDIA/EQUITY REPORTING:
Participant demographics: 196 females/82 males (70.5% female); mean age 45.6 years (SD 15.3); 203 White (74.3%), 41 African American (15.0%), 29 other races (10.6%); 29 Hispanic ethnicity (10.5%). Mean failed treatment trials: 2.85. Baseline MADRS scores ranged 20-46. No stratified analyses by demographic subgroups were reported.
