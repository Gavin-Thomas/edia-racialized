METHODS:
A double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 1b trial (October 2017-September 2020) followed by an open-label long-term extension. Forty-six participants aged 50-74 years with mild AD and confirmed amyloid pathology were enrolled at 12 sites in Canada and Europe. Participants received intrathecal BIIB080 or placebo in four dose cohorts. CSF samples were analyzed for total tau and phosphorylated tau 181 using the Elecsys platform. Tau PET imaging used [18F]-MK6240 tracer with standard uptake value ratios calculated in predefined brain regions.

RESULTS:
BIIB080 demonstrated dose-dependent reductions in CSF total tau (56% reduction in higher-dose cohorts) and CSF p-tau181 (51-56% reduction). Reductions continued through the long-term extension with quarterly dosing. Tau PET at week 25 showed reduced accumulation versus placebo. At week 100, tau PET showed reductions from baseline across all brain regions assessed, with the largest reduction in temporal composite regions (-0.71 SUVR). A moderate correlation was observed between cumulative drug exposure and tau PET change.
